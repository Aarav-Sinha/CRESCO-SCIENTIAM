Vision

Cresco Scientiam means "I grow through knowledge."

Delhi Public School Megacity built this festival on a simple frustration: most school science fairs reward neat handwriting and confident presentation more than they reward actual scientific thinking. Cresco Scientiam is our answer — twelve events, each one testing a real skill (investigation, synthesis, proof, design, argument) under real time pressure, judged by people who do that skill professionally wherever possible.

01

Rigor over recital

Every event is judged on process and reasoning, not just a final answer.

02

Real stakes

Time limits, live judging, and public results — no exercises with disappearing consequences.

03

Cross-disciplinary

Twelve domains spanning science, mathematics, strategy, music, and media.

04

Open access

Any recognized school may register — no invitation list, no legacy slots.

History

Five deployments. One growing festival.

Deployment 01 — 2022

First Ignition

Cresco Scientiam launches with four founding events — a forensics room, a chemistry challenge, a mathematics relay, and a general quiz — hosted entirely within the school's own science block.

4 events4 schools~180 participants
Deployment 02 — 2023

Beyond the Science Block

The festival expands into the auditorium and adds its first non-laboratory events — a debate chamber and a live quiz show format — while participation nearly doubles.

7 events9 schools~410 participants
Deployment 03 — 2024

Full Spectrum

Expansion to twelve distinct events spanning science, mathematics, strategy, music, and media — the lineup that stands largely unchanged today.

12 events15 schools~760 participants
Deployment 04 — 2025

Two-Day Format

Cresco Scientiam becomes a two-day festival for the first time, adding a formal opening ceremony and a dedicated awards night.

12 events21 schools~1,050 participants
Deployment 05 — 2026

Command Center Online

The fifth deployment introduces a complete digital operations system: live school registration, credentialing, real-time festival telemetry, and a role-based management console replacing what used to be a set of spreadsheets and a WhatsApp group.

Rules of Engagement

The rules that apply across every event.

R.01

Eligibility

Open to students of any recognized school, grades 9–12, registered through that school's coordinator — no individual walk-in entries.

R.02

Team composition

Team sizes are fixed per event (see each event's dossier) and may not be changed after the registration deadline without Admin approval.

R.03

Substitutions

A registered participant may be substituted up to 48 hours before the festival via a Request Permission form in the School Portal.

R.04

Materials & devices

Personal phones are not permitted inside any event room during active rounds unless the event's brief explicitly allows them.

R.05

Academic integrity

Any evidence of pre-arranged answers, external communication during a round, or plagiarized material results in immediate disqualification of the team.

R.06

Conduct

Participants, teachers, and volunteers are expected to treat judges, organizers, and each other with respect. Harassment of any kind ends participation immediately.

R.07

Judging decisions

Judging panels are drawn from subject specialists where possible. Decisions are final; written feedback is provided to every team on request.

R.08

Safety

All chemistry and engineering events run under direct teacher-in-charge supervision, with safety equipment provided at the venue.

R.09

Appeals

Disputes may be raised with the Event Head within 30 minutes of results being posted, and are escalated to Core Team if unresolved.
